Life of AR Rahman

  • 1966 born January 6, 1966, Madras

  • 1972 by age 11 he was playing piano professionally to help support his family.

  • 1988 his family converted to Islam following a sister’s recovery from a serious illness, and he then took the name Allah Rakha Rahman

  • He wrote more than 300 jingles

  • 1991Rahman met Bollywood film director Mani Ratnam, who persuaded him to write music for motion pictures

  • 1992first project was Roja,which resulted in Rahman’s first film soundtrack hit

  • 2001More than 100 movie scores followed, including the music for Lagaan

  • 2001 the first Bollywood film nominated for an Academy Award. Rahman’s albums sold more than 100 million copies.

  • 2002 Rahman composed the score for Bombay Dreams, a colourful satire of Bollywood films, and the show opened in London’s West End

  • 2004 launch of the Broadway version of the show

  • 2006 Rahman’s next stage project, a musical version of The Lord of the Rings, premiered in Toronto

  • 2010 Rahman’s streak continued at the Grammy Awards, where he collected the prize for best soundtrack and “Jai Ho”

"I love great lyrics, and I love the way it could shape a tune into a very unpredictable one, and I also like taking a great melody and putting lyrics into it."
